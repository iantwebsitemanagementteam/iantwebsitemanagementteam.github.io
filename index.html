Volunteer Role: Design Team

Masjid-Based · Agile · Ihsan-Driven

“Do you not see how Allah sets forth a parable? A good word is like a good tree—its roots are firm and its branches are high in the sky.”
— Surah Ibrāhīm, 14:24

We are forming a small design team embedded within the Muslim context in the West. This is not a conventional design opportunity, nor is it a skills-first role. It is an attempt to rethink what a design group looks like when it begins with Bismillāh—when intention, accountability, and ihsan precede tools, outputs, and recognition.

Many professional environments are technically impressive but spiritually hollow. This team exists to reverse that order: rooted in Islam first, then growing upward into excellence, professionalism, and meaningful impact.

What We Work On

  • Self-hosting infrastructure using open-source tools
  • Automation in flyer and communications design
  • Maintaining and improving the masjid website (WordPress)
  • Preparing systems and workflows for Ramadan
  • Addressing new challenges as they arise

Projects may be ongoing or seasonal.

What You Will Do

  • Execute work and contribute to real deliverables
  • Co-design solutions with others
  • Participate in skill-sharing workshops
  • Learn through iteration, reflection, and team feedback

How the Team Works

  • Report to a Product Owner / Team Lead
  • Work in a small Agile team
  • Deliver the smallest meaningful outcome (MOP)
  • Demo work early and improve through feedback
  • Use minimal resources and prioritize open-source tools
Ceremonies & Rhythm
  • Weekly 30-minute team meeting
  • Bi-weekly 20-minute sprint review
  • Less than 1 hour of ceremonies in most weeks

Time Commitment

  • 3–4 hours per week (flexible)
  • 6-month minimum commitment

Who This Is For

  • Muslim high school or college students
  • Creatives without formal training
  • Curious, ambitious individuals willing to learn

A big plus if you:

  • Read to grow in knowledge (Islamic texts and other sciences)
  • Write reflective or introspective pieces

Professionalism and commitment are non-negotiable. Deadlines are treated as an amānah.

Tools & Skills

  • Slack for communication
  • WordPress for website maintenance
  • New tools learned as required

Our operating belief is simple: anyone can learn anything.

What You Will Gain

  • Experience working in a masjid-based Islamic environment
  • Exposure to Agile workflows and team-based delivery
  • Training in design and human-centered thinking
  • Opportunities to upskill in project management, writing, design, and web development
  • Portfolio-worthy work with real impact

This is not a role built on hype or titles. It is built on roots—roots of intention, accountability, and trust in Allah. From those roots, we aim to grow something strong, useful, and lasting.
